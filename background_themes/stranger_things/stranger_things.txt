The Stranger Things color palette is deliberately cinematic and retro, drawing from 1980s horror, sci-fi, and neon signage. It is dominated by dark, moody bases with high-contrast neon accents.

Below is a commonly accepted, production-style palette that matches the show’s branding, title sequence, and key visual language.

Core Palette (Primary)

Black / Near Black

#0B0B0B

Used for: background, negative space, night scenes

Blood Red (Title Red)

#B1060F

Used for: logo, danger, Upside Down energy

This is the most iconic Stranger Things color

Dark Crimson

#6A040F

Used for: shadows, depth behind the title glow

Neon & Accent Colors (Secondary)

Neon Red Glow

#E50914

Used for: glowing text edges, light leaks, neon effects

Electric Blue

#1F3C88

Used for: sci-fi elements, night lighting, lab scenes

Cold Cyan / Teal

#2EC4B6

Used for: supernatural light, portals, electricity

Atmospheric / Supporting Colors

Fog Gray

#BFBFBF

Used for: smoke, mist, eerie ambience

Dirty Beige / Retro Paper

#E6D3A3

Used for: 80s interiors, school walls, props

Upside Down Brown-Green

#3A3F2D

Used for: decay, organic horror tones

Typical Usage Pattern

Background: Near black (#0B0B0B)

Primary focus: Blood red (#B1060F) with neon red glow (#E50914)

Contrast lighting: Electric blue (#1F3C88) or teal (#2EC4B6)

Texture & atmosphere: Grays and muted browns
